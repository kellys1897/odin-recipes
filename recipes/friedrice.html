Kimchi Fried Rice

I honestly wouldn't mind eating this everyday..

Kimchi fried rice is the perfect dish when you have leftover rice from the previous day. It's even better if you have Kimchi that has become more sour over time. This is a popular dish among Koreans for an easy at home recipe that doesn't require much!

Ingredients

  • 4 ounces of ham or a few strips of bacon that is cut into bite size pieces
  • 3/4 cup diced Kimchi
  • 1/4 small onion diced
  • 1 scallion
  • 1 small carrot, finely chopped
  • 3 -4 tablesppons of Kimchi juice in the Kimchi jar
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of Korean chili paste, gochujang
  • 2 1/2 cups of cooked rice (Older rice that was made the previous day is the best. A new batch of sticky rice won't work.
  • oil for stir frying
  • 1/2 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • salt and pepper to taste

Optional

  • 1 tsp sesame seeds
  • 2 eggs fried
  • 1 sheet of roasted gim(dried seaweed)

Steps to take

  1. If using bacon, heat a lightly oiled pan and cook bacon until brown. If using spam or ham, you can add with the kimchi.
  2. Add the onion and scallion and stir-fry quickly over high heat. Add the kimchi, optional carrot, kimchi juice, soy sauce and gochujang(Add the spam if using). Stir fry until the kimchi turns soft and into a deeper color which will take a few minutes.
  3. Add the rice and reduce the heat to medium low. Stir until it is thoroughly mixed (3-4 minutes).
  4. Once the rice is evenly coated, turn the heat to high heat and add salt or pepper to taste. Mix in the sesame oil and you can add sesame seeds to your liking.
  5. For extras, you can top the fried rice with fried eggs and gim(dried seaweed) on top.
