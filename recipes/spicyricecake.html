Korean Spicy Rice Cake

This chewy dish will make you crave for more with each bite..

Tteokbooki or Spicy Korean Rice cake is super popular in street food scene in Korea and it is something you can make easily at home! It has savory and spicy flavors with an addictive chewy texture! It was first introduced in 1953 right after the Korean war by a woman named Ma Bok-Rim. It is known to be a comfort food for an after school snack or late at night!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of tteokbokki
  • 1 sheet of eumuk (fish cake
  • 4 ounces of green cabbage
  • 1-2 scallions
  • 3 cups of anchovy broth or water
  • 3 tablespoons of Korean red chili pepper paste (gochuchang)
  • 1-3 tablespoons Korean red chili pepper flakes(gochugaru)- ideally
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of corn syrup
  • 1 tablespoon of minced garlic

Steps

  1. Make the anchovy broth. Soak the rice cakes for 20 minutes if hardened or frozen
  2. Cut the fish cake, scallions and cabbage into two inch places or to your liking.
  3. Pour the anchoby broth into a large pan on the stove over medium to high heat. Add the Korean chili paste (gochujang) and the Korean chili pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  4. Add the rice cakes into the pan. Boil until the rice cake is soft and the sauce has thickened (about 8-10 minutes. Stir frequently.
  5. Add veggies and fish cake. Continue to let it boil for 5 more minutes while stirring. Keep checking the rice cake and taste test the sauce for your liking. You can always adjust the seasoning of the dish with more water or broth. This dish is all about personalization for taste, spiciness and chewiness.
